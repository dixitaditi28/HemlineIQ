ABOUT THIS INDICATOR

The Hemline Index was first proposed by economist George Taylor in 1926. It theorises that skirt lengths shorten during periods of economic optimism and lengthen during downturns. HemlineIQ tracks this signal in real time using Google Trends search data mapped against Federal Reserve macroeconomic indicators.

A positive Hemline Index means mini skirt searches are outpacing midi and maxi — historically a signal of rising consumer confidence. A negative reading suggests the opposite.
